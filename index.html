Effects of Unemployment Rate

The Story

This map examines the effect of a country's unemployment rate on three other rates: the economic real growth rate, the population growth rate, and the literacy rate. Each option displays some interesting results - it highlights certain regions in the world that perform well, average, or poorly in each of the three rates compared to the unemployment rate.

Key

  1. Real Growth Rate: This button divides a country's real growth rate by their unemployment rate. The map displays a large green circle on countries with a high real growth rate and low unemployment rate (ratio > 1.5). The map displays a medium yellow circle on countries with a low real growth rate and average unemployment rate (ratio > 0). The map displays a small red circle for countrues with a negative real growth rate (ratio < 0).
  2. Population Growth Rate: This button divides a country's population growth rate by their unemployment rate. The map displays a large green circle on countries with a high population growth rate and low unemployment rate (ratio > 0.3). The map displays a medium yellow circle on countries with a low population growth rate and average unemployment rate (ratio > 0). The map displays a small red circle for countrues with a negative population growth rate (ratio < 0).
  3. Literacy Rate: This button divides a country's literacy rate by their unemployment rate. The map displays a large green circle on countries with a high literacy rate and low unemployment rate (ratio > 50). The map displays a medium yellow circle on countries with an average literacy rate and average unemployment rate (ratio > 15). The map displays a small red circle for countrues with a low literacy rate and high unemployment rate (ratio > 0).

Note: Circles of the same color may be of slightly different size due to the orientation of the map - their size is defined in kilometers, and this constant value is proportioned differently on different countries. Additionally, countries that do not have a circle did not have the necessary data in the file required to calculate a certain ratio.
